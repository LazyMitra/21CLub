What is 21 Club?

The most troubling development, though, has been the emerging trend of rappers dying as soon as they reach the legal drinking age. The “21 Club” is real.

Before Juice Wrld's death, there was Lil Peep, who died at age 21 from an accidental overdose in 2017, and XXXTentacion (20) and Jimmy Wopo (21), who were shot dead on the same day in 2018. Juice foretold his own death in a tribute to Peep and XXXTentacion titled “Legends.” “What’s the 27 club? We ain’t making it past 21,” he sang. Then, later in that verse: “They tell me I’mma be a legend / don’t want that title now / ’Cause all the legends seem to die out.”

The 21 Club doesn’t carry the same cultural weight as the 27 Club, but it might be one day. Juice, Peep and XXXTentacion were generational mouthpieces, pop vanguards who had already achieved a global following. While they all had various demons — XXXTentacion’s alleged domestic violence being the most controversial — they also shared much more in common than the far-flung constellation of 27 Club luminaries. They all wrote freely about mental health in a way that could verge on teen melodrama and they all possessed a considerable pop acumen that served as musical ballast. Their depressive music floated, and it resonated with millions.

Juice Wrld

Juice Wrld, whose real name was Jarad Anthony Higgins, was an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He was born on December 2, 1998, in Chicago, Illinois. From a young age, Juice Wrld showed a passion for music and began playing the piano and guitar, eventually teaching himself to produce music.

In 2015, Juice Wrld started uploading his songs to SoundCloud, gaining attention for his unique style that blended elements of hip hop, rock, and emo. He caught the attention of record labels and eventually signed with Interscope Records in 2018.

His breakout single, "Lucid Dreams," was released in 2018 and became a massive hit, peaking at number two on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. The song showcased his emotive lyrics, melodic hooks, and vulnerable storytelling, which resonated with a wide audience.

Following the success of "Lucid Dreams," Juice Wrld released his debut studio album, "Goodbye & Good Riddance," in 2018. The album received positive reviews and contained several other successful singles, including "All Girls Are the Same" and "Lean wit Me."

In 2019, Juice Wrld released his second studio album, "Death Race for Love," which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart. The album showcased his versatility as an artist, exploring various genres and displaying his lyrical prowess.

Unfortunately, Juice Wrld's promising career was tragically cut short. On December 8, 2019, at the age of 21, he suffered a seizure at Chicago's Midway International Airport and was pronounced dead shortly afterward. The cause of death was later determined to be an accidental drug overdose.

Despite his untimely passing, Juice Wrld left a significant impact on the music industry. His honest and introspective lyrics resonated with millions of fans worldwide, and his genre-blending style influenced a new generation of artists. His music continues to be celebrated and remembered as a testament to his talent and creativity.

Tragically, on June 18, 2018, XXXTentacion was shot and killed during an apparent robbery in Deerfield Beach, Florida, at the age of 20. His untimely death sent shockwaves through the music community and left a void in the industry.

XXXTentacion's music continues to resonate with fans around the world, as he was known for addressing topics such as mental health, inner struggles, and the search for meaning. His posthumous releases, including the album "Skins" and the single "Bad!", achieved significant success and solidified his impact on the industry.

Despite the controversies surrounding his personal life, XXXTentacion's artistry and willingness to address complex emotions have left a lasting legacy. His influence can be seen in the work of many contemporary artists, and his fans continue to celebrate his music and remember him as a talented and deeply introspective artist.

Pop Smoke

Pop Smoke's debut mixtape, "Meet the Woo," was released in 2019 and received positive reviews. The mixtape featured collaborations with notable artists like Travis Scott and Quavo and solidified his presence in the hip hop scene.

In 2020, Pop Smoke released his second mixtape, "Meet the Woo 2," which debuted at number seven on the Billboard 200 chart. The mixtape included the hit single "Dior," which became a chart-topping success and further propelled his career.

Tragically, on February 19, 2020, Pop Smoke was shot and killed during a home invasion in Hollywood Hills, California, at the age of 20. His death sent shockwaves through the music community, leaving fans and fellow artists devastated.

Despite his short time in the spotlight, Pop Smoke's impact on the drill and hip hop genres was significant. His deep voice, gritty lyrics, and energetic delivery brought a fresh and captivating sound to the music industry. He was seen as a rising star, and his potential was evident in his posthumous releases, including the album "Shoot for the Stars, Aim for the Moon," which achieved critical acclaim and commercial success.

Pop Smoke's music continues to resonate with fans worldwide, and his legacy as an influential artist lives on. He is remembered for his distinctive style, charismatic presence, and the promising talent he showcased during his brief but impactful career.

Jimmy Wopo

Jimmy Wopo, whose real name was Travon DaShawn Smart, was an American rapper from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. He was born on January 13, 1997. Wopo gained recognition for his gritty lyrics, charismatic delivery, and energetic style, becoming one of the rising stars in the Pittsburgh rap scene.

Growing up in the Hill District neighborhood of Pittsburgh, Wopo experienced the challenges and realities of street life. He began releasing music in 2015, capturing the attention of local listeners with his mixtapes and singles. Wopo's music often reflected the struggles, aspirations, and experiences of his community.

In 2016, Wopo gained widespread attention with his breakout single, "Elm Street," which garnered millions of views on YouTube. The song showcased his confident flow and vivid storytelling, solidifying his presence in the rap industry.

Wopo released several mixtapes throughout his career, including "Back Against the Wall" and "Jordan Kobe." His music resonated with fans who appreciated his raw honesty and ability to paint a vivid picture through his lyrics

Tragically, on June 18, 2018, Wopo was shot and killed in a drive-by shooting in Pittsburgh at the age of 21. His death shocked and saddened the rap community, leaving a void in the Pittsburgh music scene and beyond.

Jimmy Wopo's impact on the rap industry, particularly in Pittsburgh, cannot be overlooked. His talent, passion, and authenticity endeared him to his fans, who continue to celebrate his music and remember his contributions to the genre. Despite his untimely passing, Wopo's legacy lives on through his music, which serves as a testament to his artistry and the voice he brought to his community.
